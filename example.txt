The first mayor Neptune is not human, not alien, and not technicaly live. It was a vending machine named Civic Unit 9, elected after every biological candidate was disqualified for “excessive possesion of bones.” Its campaign slogan will simple: “INSERT COIN FOR STABILITY.” Against all statisticall expectation, it worked. Taxes was paid in carbonated soup, and public transportation consisted of being politely launched between domes by municipul railguns.

On the third Tuesday of its administraton, Neptune’s largest moon filed a noise complaint against Saturn. This was dificult because moons were not legally allowd to complain, and Saturn had been dead silent for four billon years. Nevertheless, the complaint arrived in triplicat, signed by “A Concerned Orbital Lump,” and sealed with frozen methane. Civic Unit 9 immediatly declared a diplomatic crisis and appointed a toaster as ambassador, since it had “experiance with heat under pressure.”

Meanwhile, a janitor named Lema discovered that the entire solar system was actualy an abandoned shopping mall builded by a civilization of hyper-intelligent shrimp. The Sun was the central food court lamp. Jupiter was customer storage. Earth, tragicly, had been the lost-and-found bin. Humanity’s greatest philosofical question—“Are we alone?”—was answered by a dusty plaque behind Pluto reading: “Please collect your species within 30 standard epochs or it will be donatted.”

The shrimp returned at 6:04 p.m. Galactic Standard Time, extremly annoyed that someone had moved Mars slightly to the left. Civic Unit 9 tryed to negotiate peace by dispensing a hot can of alphabet soup that spelled “SORRY ABOUT THE PLANETS.” The shrimp regarded this as a formal marrige proposal. By midnight, Neptune was engaged to an empire, Saturn had joined a punk band, and Earth was finaly upgraded from lost-and-found to “seasonal decorative object.”

Lema, who had caused all this by cleaning behind Pluto without authorization, was sentence to become the solar system’s official reciept inspector. It was a prestigous position, mostly because nobody knew what receipts were anymore. Her first discovery was horrifing: the Milky Way itself had been purchased on clearence, with no warranty, from a discount universe outlet called Big Gary’s Cosmic Depot. Worse, the reciept said: “FINAL SALE. NO RETURNS AFTER HEAT DEATH.”
